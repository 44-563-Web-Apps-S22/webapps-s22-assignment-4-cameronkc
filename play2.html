Act 4

Comedy-Ballet presented at Chambord, for the entertainment of the King, in the month of October 1670, and to the public in Paris for the first time at the Palais-Royal Theater 23 November 1670

CLEONTE: Ambousahim oqui boraf, Iordina, salamalequi. COVIELLE:That is to say: "Monsieur Jourdain, may your heart be all the year like a flowering rosebush." This is the way of speaking politely in those countries. I am the most humble servant of His Turkish Highness. COVIELLE: Carigar camboto oustin moraf. CLEONTE: Oustin yoc catamalequi basum base alla moran. COVIELLE: He says: "Heaven gives you the strength of lions and the wisdom of serpents." MONSIEUR JOURDAIN: His Turkish Highness honors me too much, and I wish him all sorts of good fortune. COVIELLE: Ossa binamen sadoc babally oracaf ouram. CLEONTE: Bel-men. COVIELLE: He says that you should go with him quickly to prepare yourself for the ceremony; then you can see your daughter and conclude the marriage. So many things in two words? COVIELLE: Yes; the Turkish language is like that, it says much in few words. Go quickly where he wants.
